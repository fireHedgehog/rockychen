About Me

Hi, I'm Rujia Chen, a Ph.D. in Computer and Information Sciences. My research explores cutting-edge technologies, leveraging artificial intelligence, machine learning, and audio signal processing to develop innovative solutions in musical instrument recognition and beyond.

With a background in Computer Vision and AI, I work on building advanced models that bridge the gap between theoretical research and practical applications. I'm also interested in contributing to teaching and mentoring, particularly in areas related to data science and AI. My passion lies in understanding complex patterns in data and using these insights to drive impactful innovations.

Research Focus

My primary research interests include:

  • Deep learning algorithms for musical instrument recognition
  • Semi-supervised and unsupervised learning for ecological data analysis
  • AI-driven spectrogram analysis for audio classification
  • Applications of Computer Vision in audio-visual synchronization
  • Feature extraction and optimization in audio signal processing
  • Exploring multi-modal data fusion for enhanced prediction accuracy

Technical Expertise

My expertise spans across:

  • Machine Learning & AI: Deep learning, foundation models, multimodal learning, and efficient training methods (e.g., fine-tuning, LoRA, distillation)
  • Audio & Speech Processing: Spectrogram analysis, representation learning, and generative audio models
  • Model Interpretability & Responsible AI: Explainability techniques, fairness evaluation, and ethical AI practices
  • Programming & Frameworks: Python, PyTorch, TensorFlow, Hugging Face ecosystem
  • Computer Vision: Vision transformers, object detection, and video understanding
  • Data Analysis & Visualization: Large-scale dataset handling, interactive dashboards, and advanced visualization (Plotly, Altair)
